FRAME 01 — PRE-PRODUCTION

Pre-Production

Building the Plan

Before a single frame is shot, I sit down with writers, directors, and the creative team to lock the script, the budget, and the schedule. This is where locations get secured, talent gets cast, and every piece of equipment and crew is accounted for — the quiet work that makes the loud days possible.

FRAME 02 — ON SET

Production

Running the Day

On set, my job shifts to keeping everything moving — schedules, crew, budget, and the dozen small problems that show up between calls. I try to hold the structure quietly enough that the team can stay focused on the work in front of the camera.

FRAME 03 — COORDINATION

Crew & Logistics

Keeping Everyone in Sync

A production is only as steady as its communication. Whether it's a two-person shoot or a full crew across departments, I keep the lines short between everyone who needs to know what's happening next.

FRAME 04 — WIDE

Delivery

Getting It Over the Line

By the time a project reaches post, the goal hasn't changed — deliver something that holds up creatively and commercially, on the timeline the client was promised. That's the throughline across every format I work in: television, film, digital, and live.
